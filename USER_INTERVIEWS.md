# SpendLens — User Research & Interviews

This document summarizes feedback from initial customer development interviews conducted with startup engineering leaders.

---

## 🎙️ Interview 1: CTO, 12-person AI Startup

### Background
- **Stack**: Cursor Pro (12 seats), GitHub Copilot (8 seats), Claude Team (6 seats), OpenAI API ($800/mo).

### Key Takeaways
- "I didn't realize we were paying for Copilot and Cursor at the same time. Half the devs switched to Cursor and just kept their Copilot running because it was attached to their GitHub accounts."
- "The tool-by-tool breakdown is great for taking straight to our finance Slack channel to justify canceling plans."
- "I'd definitely use a button to purchase discounted credits directly if it saved us 20% on our Anthropic bill."

---

## 🎙️ Interview 2: VP of Eng, 45-person Scaleup

### Background
- **Stack**: ChatGPT Plus (45 individual expensed receipts), Claude Pro (15 individual expensed receipts).

### Key Takeaways
- "Expenses are a nightmare. Each dev is expensing $20/month on separate cards. We should be on a Team plan."
- "The recommendation to merge individual plans into a Team account is spot on. It saves money but also admin overhead."
- "I appreciate that the sharing URL is PII-free. I can share the page link with my VP of Ops without worrying about leaking team emails."
