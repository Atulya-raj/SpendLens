# SpendLens — Go-To-Market Strategy (GTM)

This document outlines the positioning, target personas, distribution channels, and launch strategies for SpendLens.

---

## 🎯 Product Positioning

- **Value Proposition**: "Stop guessing your AI spend. Audit your stack in 2 minutes for free."
- **Core Hook**: A lightweight, self-serve tool requiring no integrations or auth, serving as a lead generator for Credex.
- **Target Persona**:
  - **CTOs & VPs of Engineering**: Responsible for tooling budgets, tired of manual seat checks.
  - **Finance Managers / Ops**: Spotting high SaaS charges but lacking technical context (e.g., they don't know that Cursor has Copilot features built-in).

---

## 📣 Distribution Channels

### 1. Product Hunt Launch
- Launch as a free utility.
- Hook: Share a screenshot of your audit report's savings card.

### 2. Cold Outreach & Social Selling (Twitter/X & LinkedIn)
- Reach out to CTOs posting about "high Claude / API bills."
- Provide value by linking to SpendLens: "Hey, we built a free tool to audit that stack. Takes 2 mins, no auth."
- Encourage users to tweet their savings directly from the dashboard.

### 3. Engineering Communities
- Share on Hacker News, Reddit (r/startups, r/saas), and CTO Slack groups.
- Position the tool as open, transparent, and built on community rules.

---

## 🧲 Lead Conversion (To Credex)

The primary success metric for SpendLens is **Credex Qualified Leads**.
- **The Hook**: When a user's audit reveals over $200/mo in savings, the inline `LeadCapture` component offers to buy subscription credits at a discount.
- **Integration**: Leads are routed to Credex agents for high-touch onboarding.
